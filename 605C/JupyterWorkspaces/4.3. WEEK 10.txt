*From:* Gemini 3 Flash *Date:* Thu Apr 02 2026 14:27:24 GMT-0600 (Mountain Day- light Time) Here are questions for graduate students to explore using SageMath:

1. Genus Growth: Calculate the genus of X_0(N) and X_1(N) for N [1,100]. For which N is the genus 0? When does X_0(N) first become hyperelliptic (genus \geq 2)?

2. Cusp Geometry: Use 'G.cusps()' and 'G.cosets()'. For \Gamma_0(N), verify the formula for the number of cusps: \sum_{d\mid N}\phi(gcd(d,N/d)).

3. Dimension Formulas: Compare the output of 'G.dimension_cusp forms(k)' with the Riemann-Roch formula. For a fixed N, verify that the dimension grows linearly with k.

4. Index and Subgroups: Calculate the index [\SL_2(\mathbb{Z}):\Gamma_0(N)] and [\SL_2(\mathbb{Z}):\Gamma_1(N)]. Verify that the ratio is \phi(N)/2 for N > 2.

5. Fundamental Domains: Plot the fundamental domains for \Gamma_0(p) for various primes p. How does the number of side-pairings relate to the generators found by 'G.generators()'?

6. Elliptic Points: Use 'G.nu2()' and 'G.nu3() to count elliptic points of order 2 and 3. How do these points affect the genus formula for X_0(N)?

7. Comparison of \Gamma(N) types: For a fixed N, compare the dimensions of S_k(\Gamma_0(N)), S_k(\Gamma_1(N))and S_k(\Gamma(N)). Which space is the largest and why?

8. Hecke Operators: (Extension) Use 'CuspForms(G, k).hecke_matrix(p)' to find the eigenvalues of T_p. Can you find cases where the characteristic polynomial is irreducible?
