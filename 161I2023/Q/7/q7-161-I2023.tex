\begin{Ej}
 Find the value of the sum $\frac{4!}{0!(4-0)!}+\frac{4!}{1!(4-1)!}+\frac{4!}{2!(4-2)!}+\frac{4!}{3!(4-3)!}+\frac{4!}{4!(4-4)!}$. Show your work by calculating the factorials in question.
\end{Ej}
\begin{ptcb}
First we find the values of the factorials in question:
$$0!=1,\ 1!=1,\ 2!=2,\ 3!=6,\ 4!=24.$$
The sum in question is then 
$$\frac{24}{1\.24}+\frac{24}{1\.6}+\frac{24}{2\.2}+\frac{24}{6\.1}+\frac{24}{24\.1}=1+4+6+4+1=16.$$
\end{ptcb}

\begin{Ej}
 Suppose we wish to form a committee (president, secretary, treasurer, attorney and communicator) out of five people, $U,V,X,Y$ and $Z$. In how many ways can this be done? (You may leave your answer in terms of factorials.)\par 
 With this in hand, explain what does the factorial mean? (\emph{It's the number of ways such that\dots})
\end{Ej}

\begin{ptcb}
  There are 5 positions to be held in the committee, the first slot can be held by any of the five people, so $5$ options. The second slot can be held by any of the four remaining people. Proceeding in that way we see that there are 
  $$5\.4\.3\.2\.1=5!\ \text{ways to form the committee.}$$
  In this sense, the factorial is the number of ways to arrange $n$ distinct objects into $n$ distinct slots.
\end{ptcb}

\begin{Ej}
  Compute the limit $\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{e^{\frac{\log(n)}{2}}}{n}$.
\end{Ej}

\begin{ptcb}
  We may first notice that $e^{\frac{\log(n)}{2}}=\left(e^{\log(n)}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}}=\sqrt{n}$ so we may simplify the expression in the limit to 
  $$\frac{\sqrt{n}}{n}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{n}}\xrightarrow[n\to\infty]{}0.$$
\end{ptcb}


\begin{Ej}
  Compute the limit $\lim_{n\to\infty}\cos\left(\frac{n!}{n^n}\right)$.
\end{Ej}

\begin{ptcb}
As cosine is a continuous function it suffices to find the limit of the sequence inside and then plug it into the cosine. The limit in question is 
$$\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{n!}{n^n}=0,\ \text{because }n!\ll n^n.$$
So the value of our expression is $\cos(0)=1$.
\end{ptcb}
